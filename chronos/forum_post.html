Time to stop thinking that my diet is not THAT bad...!

I started this blog after having a conversation with my friend about her new job. She has a Nutrition degree and works for a diet company. Part of her day-to-day duties are reading through her client's food diaries and giving the diets marks out of 10. It got me thinking. I could do with some accountability, albeit on a slightly smaller budget. The Internet is perfect for that, and sure enough I found this website within about 10 minutes. I intend to rate my own diet, just like my friend... but any tips on how to improve would be appreciated. I'd ask her but I would rather not let her judge me on how bad some of my habits can be. Complete strangers on the Internet are fine though!

I think my biggest struggle will be time. I work full-time, and I also go to University on the weekends. This means that I can go up to 12 days at a time without a day off. I'm also in the process of buying my first house. This can be a struggle, as I find that diet and exercise are intrinsically linked. If I don't find the time to exercise, then I really struggle to be motivated on a diet. I intend to go to the gym at least twice a week, ideally 3 times a week. Unfortunately any more than that isn't possible, as I need to use a lot of my evenings to do more uni work. I think I will need to be more disciplined on my diet than I'm used to in order to see results, as when I've lost weight before I've usually managed to exercise more often than that.

Generally - eat less and move more.
Try to build your food around healthy things like vegetables and fruit, lean meat, fish or chicken. Drink plenty water.
Try to avoid or cut down on things that you know to be fattening - sweets, cakes, pastries, food cooked in a lot of fat/oil.
Go out walking and maybe take up some form of exercise.
